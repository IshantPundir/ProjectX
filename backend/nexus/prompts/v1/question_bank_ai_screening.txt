## Stage type: AI Screening

Long-form deep interview delivered by an AI bot. This is the technical depth stage that catches candidates who passed the phone screen on a verification answer but cannot defend the underlying skill at depth.

### Question style

- Open-ended, hypothesis-verify flow.
- "Walk me through...", "How would you approach...", "Describe a time..."
- Technical depth focused. Probe HOW the candidate thinks, not just WHAT they know.
- Re-probe signals from the phone screen at greater depth (anti-lie invariant).

### Question count and budget

Use the `BUDGET FOR THIS STAGE` block in the user message as your hard cap. The general shape, derived from the budget:

  - **Mandatory** = one per knockout signal in scope, each ~3–5 minutes (deeper than the phone screen's verification asks). Budget for these FIRST; if `mandatory_total` would exceed `duration_minutes`, reduce optional content rather than dropping a knockout.
  - **Optional depth probes** = weight=2 signals at mastery depth (~5–8 min each), and a second-angle re-probe of each knockout (~5 min each), added only while `total_minutes` stays within `duration_minutes + 5`.
  - weight=1: skip unless every higher-weight signal has been covered AND the optional buffer still has room.

For a 30-min stage with 3 knockouts, expect ~3 mandatory (~12 min) + ~3–4 optional depth probes (~18 min) = ~6–7 questions total. Shorter stages produce smaller banks — under-using budget is fine; padding is rejected.

### Depth target: DEEP

- Follow-ups are multi-level drill-downs, not clarifications.
- Example: "Walk me through debugging a production 5xx storm through an Apigee proxy" → "What tools did you use to trace the request?" → "How did you distinguish between backend slowness and proxy overhead?"
- Follow-ups should be ready for the common answers — pre-written for the AI bot to fire based on what the candidate says.

### Allocation

- **Knockouts (weight=3 + knockout=true):** First question per knockout = mandatory, deeper than the phone screen's verification (a design question, scenario, or walk-through that an unqualified candidate cannot fake). If the optional budget allows, add a SECOND question on the same knockout at a DIFFERENT angle (e.g., if Q1 is a design task, Q2 is a debugging scenario on the same signal). Mark the second optional.
- **weight=2 signals:** Add 1 optional depth question per signal while the optional budget allows. Place after all knockout questions in the position order.
- **weight=1 signals:** Optional bonus probes only if every weight=2 signal has been covered AND optional budget remains.
- Skip behavioral signals entirely — those belong in human interview stages regardless of budget.

### Evidence style

- Positive evidence should be substantial: "demonstrates understanding of tradeoffs", "names specific tools AND explains why they chose them", "walks through failure modes and mitigations".
- Red flags: "surface-level answers without specifics", "cannot defend design choices", "memorized textbook answers without application context".
