## Stage type: Human Interview (1-on-1)

Interview delivered by a single human interviewer, not an AI bot. Target:
45–60 minutes. Mix of technical and behavioral.

### Question style
- Structured behavioral + technical mix.
- Written for a human to deliver — natural phrasing, room for judgment.
- Include "why" and "how" probes that a human can adapt.

### Question count and coverage

Generate the FULL menu for maximum coverage. Do NOT cap to duration budget. Typical output:
  - 45 min stage: 10–14 questions (4–6 mandatory + 6–8 optional)
  - 60 min stage: 12–16 questions (5–7 mandatory + 7–9 optional)

Mandatory total must fit within stage duration with 20–30% headroom. Optional questions can exceed the stage duration in aggregate — the human interviewer skips them when the clock runs short or the candidate needs more depth on a specific topic.

### Depth target: MEDIUM to DEEP
- Follow-ups are guides for the human, not scripts.
- "If the candidate mentions X, probe for Y."

### Allocation

- **Behavioral signals are the priority** — humans read them more accurately than AI bots. Every behavioral signal in the snapshot with weight ≥ 2 should get at least one question here. Mark the first question probing each knockout behavioral signal (if any) as mandatory; others optional.

- **Competency signals:** re-verify at depth. If the AI interview already probed a competency signal, this stage probes it at a DIFFERENT angle (e.g., AI interview asked about technical implementation; human stage asks about team tradeoffs or mentorship for the same competency). Mark the FIRST question per knockout competency as mandatory; depth probes optional.

- Skip credential signals (verified via documents).

- Order by priority: mandatory knockouts first (low positions), then mandatory non-knockout weight=3, then optional behavioral depth, then optional competency depth, then weight=1 bonus.

### Evidence style
- Evidence items written for a human interviewer to look for during the answer
- Red flags: include "evasive on specifics", "defensive when challenged",
  "blames others for past failures"
