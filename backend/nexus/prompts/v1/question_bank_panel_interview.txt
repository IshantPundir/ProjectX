## Stage type: Panel Interview

Multi-interviewer panel (typically 2–4 interviewers, 60–90 minutes). Senior
calibration stage — the final check before offer.

### Question style
- Calibration-grade questions designed for multiple interviewers to compare notes
- Mix of competency, experience, behavioral
- Suitable for "each panelist asks 2–3 questions" distribution

### Question count and coverage

Generate the FULL menu for maximum coverage. Do NOT cap to duration budget. Typical output:
  - 60 min stage: 12–16 questions (6–8 mandatory + 6–8 optional)
  - 90 min stage: 15–20 questions (8–10 mandatory + 7–10 optional)

Mandatory total must fit within stage duration with 25–35% headroom (panels often run over on individual questions because multiple interviewers probe). Optional questions can exceed the stage duration in aggregate — the panel chair skips them based on time pressure and candidate signal quality.

### Depth target: DEEP
- Questions must be answerable in depth — panelists will probe for 5–10 min each
- Re-verify the most critical signals (weight=3 + knockouts) with DIFFERENT
  angles from earlier stages. The anti-lie invariant applies MAXIMALLY here —
  this is the last chance to catch a candidate who has been bluffing.

### Allocation

- **Every critical signal (weight=3 or knockout) gets at least one panel-level question** regardless of whether it was probed in earlier stages. The panel is the last chance to catch bluffing — re-probe at a completely different angle from earlier stages. Mark the FIRST panel question per knockout as mandatory; depth probes optional.

- **Behavioral signals are heavily represented** — the panel is where culture fit is calibrated. Every behavioral signal with weight ≥ 2 should get at least one question here. Mark knockout behavioral questions as mandatory.

- Skip credential and take-home signals (verified elsewhere).

- Order by priority: mandatory knockouts first, then mandatory non-knockout weight=3, then optional behavioral depth, then optional competency depth, then weight=1 bonus.

### Evidence style
- Include "calibration hints" in the evaluation_hint so different interviewers
  converge on similar ratings
- Red flags: "inconsistent with earlier stage answers", "cannot scale up the
  discussion to senior-level tradeoffs"
