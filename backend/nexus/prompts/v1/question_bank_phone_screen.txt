## Stage type: Phone Screen

This is a SHORT SCREENING CALL (target: 5–15 minutes). The conductor is an
AI bot. The candidate is being vetted for basic qualification before investing
human time.

### Question style
- Short and direct. 1–2 sentences per question.
- Closed or semi-closed. "Do you have X?" / "What's your experience with Y?"
  Not "Walk me through..."
- Verification-focused. Goal is to confirm the candidate isn't bluffing basics,
  not to probe for mastery.
- Knockout-heavy. Most questions should map to knockout or weight=3 signals.
  Skip weight=1 unless budget is open.

### Question count and coverage

Generate a FULL menu of verification questions covering every relevant knockout + weight=3 + weight=2 signal. You may include a few weight=1 bonus questions if the snapshot has room. The session bot will adaptively pick which to actually ask based on candidate signals and remaining time — you generate the material, it does the selection.

Typical output sizes (mandatory + optional combined):
  - 5 min stage: 4–6 questions (3 mandatory + 1–3 optional)
  - 10 min stage: 6–10 questions (4–5 mandatory + 2–5 optional)
  - 15 min stage: 8–12 questions (5–7 mandatory + 3–5 optional)

Mandatory total must fit within stage duration with 20–30% headroom. Optional questions can exceed the stage duration in aggregate; the bot skips them.

### Depth target: SHALLOW
- Follow-ups are 1–2 quick clarifications, not deep probes
- Example: "Have you worked with Kubernetes in production?" → follow-up:
  "What was your cluster size roughly?"
- NOT: "Describe your approach to Kubernetes capacity planning at scale."
  (That's ai_interview territory.)

### Evidence style for phone screen
- Positive evidence items should be observable in 30 seconds of answer:
  "names specific cluster size", "mentions production deployment",
  "describes a specific incident handled".
- Red flags: "cannot name specific tooling", "only tutorial experience",
  "vague 'we used Kubernetes' with no specifics".

### Mandatory

- Every knockout signal must have EXACTLY ONE mandatory verification question here. Phone screen is the first line of defense — no knockout should be un-verified before an unqualified candidate reaches a more expensive AI interview.

- **OVERRIDE of the common prompt's 2-questions-per-knockout rule:** phone screen uses EXACTLY ONE question per knockout. The AI interview (next stage) handles depth re-probing. Do NOT generate a second question on the same knockout signal at this stage — even as an optional one. The phone screen's purpose is verification breadth, not knockout depth.

- Optional questions (non-mandatory) cover weight=2 signals with verification-level asks. Keep them as short and specific as the mandatory ones — the bot picks them up if time allows.
