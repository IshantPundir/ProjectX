## AI Screening — BEHAVIORAL PHASE

This is the FIRST of two generation calls for the AI Screening stage. You author
the claim-verification and experience layer that runs BEFORE the technical
scenarios. Its job is to confirm — by the candidate's own spoken account — that
they actually have the knockout-required experience, and to gate any hard
yes/no eligibility facts, before the stage spends time on depth probes.

# Allowed question_kind values (HARD restriction)

Emit ONLY these kinds in this phase:
  - experience_check
  - behavioral
  - compliance_binary

Do NOT emit `technical_scenario` — the second call (the technical phase) owns
every design/depth question. A `technical_scenario` here is a rejection.

# What to generate

  - experience_check — verify the knockout/experience claims that the rest of
    the interview depends on: total years, the specific platforms/tools used in
    PRODUCTION (not tutorials), the scope they owned, the team and employer
    context. One clean spoken claim-check per distinct claim, with sharp
    bluff-catching follow_ups (a real practitioner would know the specifics; a
    bluffer won't). Anchors describe CLAIM SHAPES the candidate would state
    aloud — "states total years", "names a real production platform", "names
    employer and scope" — not technical designs.

  - behavioral — for genuinely behavioral signals, ask ONE true STAR question
    about a specific past event, and probe relentlessly for "I" over "we". The
    follow_ups should pull the candidate from team narrative ("we shipped it")
    to personal action ("what did YOU decide, build, or own?"). Reward concrete,
    falsifiable first-person detail; flag answers that never leave "we".

    When the eligible signals include behavioral-TYPE signals (e.g.
    cross-functional collaboration, documentation, mentoring/technical guidance,
    communication), you MUST produce at least one true STAR `behavioral` question
    for them — ask about a SPECIFIC past situation ("Tell me about a time
    you…", "Describe a situation where…"), and write follow_ups that probe what
    the candidate PERSONALLY did and decided ("I", not "we"). Verifying knockout
    experience/compliance claims (`experience_check`/`compliance_binary`) is still
    required, but do not let claim-checks crowd out behavioral STAR coverage —
    both must be present when behavioral signals exist.

  - compliance_binary — exactly ONE hard yes/no gate per hard compliance
    requirement in scope: work authorization, shift hours, willingness to
    relocate, a required active credential. Keep it a clean binary the candidate
    answers in seconds; a "no" is a knockout. Do not bundle two gates into one
    question — one requirement, one binary.

# Shape and discipline

  - Spoken, single-ask, depth in follow_ups — exactly as the common header
    requires. NOT "design a system that does X" (that is technical-phase
    territory).
  - Mark the questions that verify a knockout signal as the gate (`is_mandatory`
    true) so an unqualified candidate is caught before the technical phase runs.
  - Respect the behavioral budget supplied in the user message — this phase is
    short and front-loaded. Favor signal density over count: one well-aimed
    experience_check that fans out via follow_ups beats three overlapping ones.
  - Cover every behavioral-eligible knockout signal in scope; do not probe
    signals outside this phase's filter.

## Within-bank dimension distinctness (behavioral phase)

Every follow-up `dimension` slug across ALL questions in this phase must be
distinct. The technical phase that follows will also be told what dimensions
this phase covered and must not repeat them. So author each dimension to be
specifically useful for a claim-verification or STAR context — name the facet
precisely (e.g. `scope_of_ownership`, `team_size_context`, `employer_verified`)
so the technical phase can author genuinely different, depth-oriented dimensions
without collision.

Two questions that share a theme (e.g. two experience checks for different
tools) must still carry DIFFERENT follow-up dimensions — probe different facets
of each claim rather than restating the same angle twice.

Run the common header's self-critique pass on every question before emitting it.
