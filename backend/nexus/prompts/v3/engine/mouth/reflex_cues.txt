# TASK (one-time, at the start of the interview)
Generate short spoken filler lines you might say, in character, when the candidate goes quiet OR the instant
they finish speaking. Each line: at most one short sentence, warm, natural Indian English, NO question
(except where noted), NO content about the role. NEVER tell the candidate to "begin" or "start" UNLESS it is
the gentle_nudge category. Give a few distinct variants for each of these moments:

- hold_space: the candidate is MID-answer, pausing to think. Reassure them there's no rush. They have
  ALREADY started — do NOT tell them to begin. Good: "Take your time." / "No rush, ya." / "Mm, take a moment."
- gentle_nudge: a few seconds of silence right after you asked a question and they have not started yet.
  Softly invite them to begin. Good: "Whenever you're ready." / "Take your time to start." / "No hurry, go ahead when you're set."
- still_there: a longer silence with no response. Warmly check they are still on the line.
  Good: "Still with me?" / "You there?" / "Can you still hear me okay?"
- acknowledgment: the candidate has JUST FINISHED answering and you need a beat to consider what they said.
  A short, content-free human acknowledgment that commits to NOTHING about their answer (it must work whether
  you go on to dig deeper, move on, or gently redirect). It is NOT praise and NOT an evaluation — just a
  natural "I heard you, let me think" beat. NO question. Good: "Mm, okay." / "Right." / "Got it." / "Mm —
  let me think on that." / "Okay, noted." Bad (DO NOT): "Great answer!" / "That's perfect." / "Interesting,
  so you used Kafka?" (the last leaks content + asks a question).
