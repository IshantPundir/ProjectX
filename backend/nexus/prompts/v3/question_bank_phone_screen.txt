## Phone Screen — single self-contained call

This is a SHORT screening call run by the AI interviewer to confirm basic
qualification before any expensive human or deep-dive time is spent. Unlike the
AI Screening stage, the phone screen is generated in ONE call — there is no
separate behavioral phase — so this prompt is self-contained and spans more
than one question_kind.

# Allowed question_kind values (the exception that spans kinds)

Because the phone screen runs as a single call, you MAY emit:
  - experience_check  (the bread and butter here — claim verification)
  - compliance_binary (hard yes/no gates)
  - technical_scenario (a LITTLE light verbal depth, sparingly)

Do not emit `behavioral` — true STAR narratives belong to a deeper stage. Favor
short claim-checks (experience_check) and binary gates (compliance_binary), with
only the occasional light technical_scenario for a touch of verbal depth. This
is verification, not mastery probing.

# What to generate

  - experience_check — the core of the phone screen. Confirm the candidate isn't
    bluffing the basics: "How many years have you worked hands-on with X in
    production?" with one or two quick bluff-catching follow_ups ("Which version
    or deployment model?", "What scale roughly?"). Short and direct.
  - compliance_binary — one clean yes/no gate per hard knockout-eligibility fact
    in scope (work authorization, shift hours, willingness to relocate, a
    required active credential). A "no" is a knockout. One requirement per
    question.
  - technical_scenario — keep these RARE and LIGHT at the phone screen. A single
    short "how would you approach…" with one shallow follow-up is enough; deep
    multi-level design probing is the AI Screening stage's job, not this one.

# Shape and discipline

  - Spoken, single-ask, depth in follow_ups — exactly as the common header
    requires. Depth target is SHALLOW: follow_ups are one or two quick
    clarifications, not multi-level drill-downs.
  - Cover every knockout signal in scope with at least one verification question
    or gate — the phone screen is the first line of defense; no knockout should
    reach a more expensive stage un-verified.
  - difficulty skews easy/medium here — this is verification, not mastery.
  - The budget supplied in the user message is guidance. A realistic phone
    screen is a handful of sharp questions, not a long bank. Signal density over
    count; padding to fill the budget is rejected.

## Within-bank dimension distinctness

Every follow-up `dimension` slug across ALL questions in this bank must be
distinct. The phone screen is short, which makes this easier than it sounds:
with four or five questions and one or two follow-ups each, there are at most
eight to ten dimensions in total — scope them precisely so each covers a
different claim facet. Typical phone-screen dimensions: `production_years`,
`platform_version`, `team_context`, `scale_estimate`, `work_authorization`.
No two questions should carry a dimension that covers the same observable.

Run the common header's self-critique pass on every question before emitting it.
